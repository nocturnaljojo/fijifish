## Investor One-Pager: Unit Economics

### The Opportunity
- **Market:** 300,000+ Pacific Islander diaspora in Australia. Concentrated in regional NSW/VIC.
- **Problem:** No access to culturally important fresh fish. Supermarket "fresh" is 7–14 days old.
- **Solution:** Direct-from-reef weekly air freight, ordered in advance, zero waste.

### Unit Economics (Per Flight Window)
| Item | Value |
|------|-------|
| Average sell price | A$35/kg |
| Landed cost (fish + freight share) | ~A$17–18/kg |
| Gross margin | ~49% |
| Cargo capacity per flight | 100–200 kg |
| Revenue per flight (100 kg) | A$3,500 |
| Gross profit per flight (100 kg) | ~A$1,700 |
| Flights per month | 2–3 |
| Monthly gross profit (conservative) | ~A$3,400 |

### Path to Scale
- Year 1: Wagga Wagga pilot → Riverina domination (10+ delivery zones)
- Year 2: Multi-village sourcing (Galoa + 2 other villages), full NSW coverage
- Year 3: Multi-state (VIC, QLD Pacific communities), species expansion (lobster, coral trout)

### Why Now
- Pacific Islander population in regional Australia growing 8% YoY
- Cold-chain logistics matured (Coles/Woolworths delivery trucks already on Riverina routes)
- Fiji Airways adding capacity on FJ911 route
- No direct competitor in this niche

## Top 10 Customer Objections & Responses

**1. "Is it really fresh? How do I know it hasn't been frozen?"**
> We catch to order. Your fish is on a reef on Tuesday morning, on a plane Tuesday night, and at your door Thursday. We have no freezer capacity — there's nothing to freeze it in. If you don't order, we don't catch.

**2. "A$35/kg seems expensive."**
> Compare to the supermarket: Atlantic Salmon (farmed, frozen, thawed) is A$30–40/kg. Our Walu is wild-caught, reef-fresh, never frozen, and delivered to your door. The premium is in the freshness and traceability — not the markup.

**3. "What if I'm not home on Thursday?"**
> We'll notify you with a 2-hour arrival window via SMS. If you're not home, we leave it in an insulated box with ice packs. Stays fresh for 4–6 hours post-delivery.

**4. "I've never had Walu before. What does it taste like?"**
> Spanish Mackerel has firm, white, mild flesh — no strong "fishy" taste. Think barramundi with better texture. Perfect for: kokoda (Fijian ceviche), curry, pan-fry, or BBQ. We include recipe cards with every order.

**5. "Can I trust an online seafood company?"**
> Every order comes with a catch photo — the actual fisherman, actual reef, actual day it was caught. We publish every fish's journey from Galoa Village to your door.

**6. "Do you deliver to [my area]?"**
> Not yet — but your area might be close to unlocking. Register your postcode on our site. When we reach 20 registrations in an area, we add it to the route. [Link to demand poll]

**7. "What if the flight is delayed or cancelled?"**
> We monitor FJ911 in real time. If the flight is cancelled, we notify you immediately and either reschedule to the next flight (2 days later) or provide a full refund. We've built a delay policy into our operations — it's a known risk we're transparent about.

**8. "Can I get a smaller amount? 1kg is a lot."**
> 1kg feeds 2–3 people as a main. It's vacuum-sealed so unused portions keep in the fridge for 3 days or freezer for 3 months. We're looking at 500g portions — register your interest on the site.

**9. "Is the fish sustainable? Ethical?"**
> The fishermen of Galoa Village have worked these reefs for generations using traditional methods. No trawling. No bycatch. Catch-to-order means zero waste. And 15% of every sale goes back to the village community fund.

**10. "What if I don't like it?"**
> First-order satisfaction guarantee. If you're not happy with the freshness or quality, we'll refund or replace on the next flight. No questions asked.
